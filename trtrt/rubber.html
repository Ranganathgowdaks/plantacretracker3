Rubber plant

The rubber plant, scientifically known as Ficus elastica, is a popular and adaptable houseplant celebrated for its bold and glossy foliage. Characterized by large, leathery leaves with a deep green hue, the rubber plant adds a touch of lushness to indoor spaces. Its name is derived from the latex it produces, which was historically used in the production of rubber. This plant is highly valued for its ease of care, thriving in a variety of light conditions, from bright indirect sunlight to lower light levels. Rubber plants prefer well-draining soil and require moderate watering, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings to prevent root rot. With its air-purifying qualities, the rubber plant contributes to a healthier indoor environment by removing pollutants such as formaldehyde. Its upright growth habit and the potential for significant height make it a striking addition to home decor. Propagation is possible through stem cuttings, providing an opportunity for enthusiasts to share this beautiful plant. As a pet-friendly option, the rubber plant is safe for households with animals, making it a versatile and beloved choice for indoor greenery.
